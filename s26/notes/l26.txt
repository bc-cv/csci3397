**[10 min] 1. What Are Foundation Models?**
- **Core Concept**: Large-scale models pretrained on broad data, then adapted to downstream tasks.
- **The Pretrain-Then-Adapt Paradigm**:
  - Pretraining: Learn general representations from massive (often unlabeled) datasets.
  - Adaptation: Fine-tune or prompt for specific tasks (classification, segmentation, etc.).
- **Why Now?**: Convergence of data scale, compute (GPUs/TPUs), and algorithmic advances.
- **Key Distinction**: Task-specific models vs. one model adapted to many tasks.

**[10 min] 2. Scaling Laws & Emergent Capabilities**
- **Scaling Dimensions**: Model size (parameters), dataset size, compute budget.
- **Empirical Scaling Laws**: Predictable performance gains with scale (Kaplan et al.).
- **Emergent Behaviors**: Capabilities that appear only at sufficient scale (e.g., in-context learning).
- **Trade-offs**: Compute cost, carbon footprint, diminishing returns at extreme scale.

**[10 min] 3. Pretraining Data & Strategies**
- **Data Sources**: Web-crawled text/images, curated datasets, domain-specific corpora.
- **Pretraining Objectives**:
  - Language: Masked language modeling (BERT), autoregressive (GPT).
  - Vision: Masked image modeling (MAE), contrastive learning (CLIP).
  - Multimodal: Image-text alignment, vision-language pretraining.
- **Data Quality vs. Quantity**: Filtering, deduplication, and curation matter.

**[10 min] 4. Benchmarks & Evaluation**
- **General Vision Benchmarks**: ImageNet, COCO, ADE20K.
- **Biomedical Benchmarks**:
  - MedMNIST: Standardized collection of 2D/3D biomedical image classification tasks.
  - CheXpert: Large-scale chest X-ray dataset for pathology detection.
- **Evaluation Paradigms**: Zero-shot, few-shot, linear probing, full fine-tuning.
- **Why Benchmarks Matter**: Reproducibility, fair comparison, tracking progress.
